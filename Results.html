NO2 and PM 2.5 Annual Averages (2013-2022)

From the 10-year analysis of both pollutants, it can be seen that their trends are fairly consistent, with NO2 almost always remaining at low concentrations across the entire country, while PM 2.5 maintains high concentrations over nearly the entire national territory. As shown on the map, there is only one area where both pollutants overlap almost perfectly, which corresponds to the capital. The analysis of the graphs further highlights the difference between the two pollutants, as NO2 never exceeds the EU average and always remains below the limits set by the WHO. PM 2.5, on the other hand, although its annual average remains below European limits, has in recent years repeatedly recorded exceedances of the standards.



Pollution during the different season

We can observe that, due to its low levels, NO2 does not show significant variation throughout the year between the different seasons, whereas PM 2.5 clearly increases during the winter period, likely due to heating systems.

Exposure of the population to pollution levels and comparison with WHO limits

These representations show that both pollutants have higher concentrations near urban centers, once again reminding us how human activities play a crucial role in the increase of airborne particles.
