Pollution during the different season.

Analysis of air pollution patterns during summer and winter, highlighting seasonal differences in pollutant concentrations.

  • Seasonal maps show clear differences in PM 2.5 and NO2 levels.
  • Winter months generally exhibit higher concentrations due to heating and atmospheric conditions.
  • Summer patterns are influenced by photochemical reactions and reduced emissions.

Difference between PM 2.5 and NO2

Comparative overview of the spatial and temporal distribution of PM 2.5 and NO2 pollutants.

  • PM 2.5 and NO2 originate from different sources and have distinct spatial patterns.
  • PM 2.5 shows more variability over the years, while NO2 remains relatively stable.
  • Health impacts and regulatory limits differ for each pollutant.
