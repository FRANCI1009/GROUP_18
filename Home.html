Air Pollution in Bosnia and Herzegovina (2013–2022)

This platform presents the results of a research project analyzing air pollution trends across Bosnia and Herzegovina using open-source geospatial data and WebGIS technologies. It combines atmospheric models, population grids, and land cover data to assess the exposure of citizens to NO₂, PM2.5, and PM10 over the last decade, highlighting key urban and environmental dynamics.

  • Understanding the Spatial Patterns of Pollution

    Air pollution distribution is not uniform. Our maps reveal how concentrations vary between urban centers and rural regions, with major hotspots around Sarajevo, Banja Luka, and Tuzla.

  • The Role of Seasonality and Heating Practices

    Winter months show significantly higher pollutant levels, mainly due to widespread use of solid fuels for residential heating. December is the most critical period across the country.

  • Connecting Pollution to Population and Land Cover

    By combining pollution data with population density and land use, we identify which groups are most exposed and which areas are environmentally at risk, guiding urban and territorial planning.
